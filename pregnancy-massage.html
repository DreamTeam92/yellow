Pregnancy Massage

 

First and foremost, prenatal massage makes you feel good! It nurtures and pampers the body at a time when it needs it the most. In addition to soothing the body and dissolving stress, a trained touch delivers a wide range of psychological and emotional benefits. It’s a safe and relaxing way to relieve pregnancy’s common discomforts like backache, headaches, heartburn, fatigue, carpal tunnel syndrome, leg cramps, oedema, nasal congestion, sciatica and shortness of breath.

 

Prenatal massage not only benefits the mother but also your baby too. Everything a pregnant woman does, from her diet and fitness to her relaxation and relative state of contentment – also directly affects the child she is carrying. Study after study has proven that calmness in the mother leads to healthier in-utero development.

 

Regular prenatal massage can help prepare you for labour by making the pelvis more supple and creates more awareness within your body for relaxation.

 

Just like a regular massage, prenatal massage:

  • Improves circulation
  • Eases neck, back, and joint pain
  • Relieves muscle spasms and cramps
  • Promotes relaxation
  • Raises endorphin levels

 

Prenatal massage also helps to:

 

  • Remove toxins
  • Improve quality of sleep
  • Reduce swelling/oedema in joints
  • Regulate stress hormones
  • Aid lymphatic drainage

 

Precautions for Prenatal Massage

 

The following conditions are reason to avoid massage during pregnancy:

 

  • High-risk pregnancy
  • High blood pressure
  • Pre-eclampsia
  • Pre-term contractions
  • Previous pre-term labor
  • Severe swelling
  • Severe headache
